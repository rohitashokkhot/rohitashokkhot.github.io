Echidna

The Echidna ranges from 35-52 cm in length and can weigh up t 6kg. Echidnas are covered with fur that colours that range from reddish to dark brown.

Scientific Name: Tachyglossus aculeatus
    Diet: Ants and termites though they have been known to eat other invertebrates.
      Habitat: The Echidna is usually found in open heathland, forests, woodlands, scrublands and grasslands, among vegetation or in hollow logs.
        Did you know? A baby Echidna is called a ‘puggle’.

        Kangaroo

        Kangaroos can be up to 1.5 m tall and weigh almost 60 kg. Their fur is grey brown in colour. Their underparts are pale grey or whitish. They have long ears with a whitish inner fringe and dark eyes.

        Scientific Name: Macropus giganteus
          Diet: Grasses, herbs and occasionally shrubs.
            Habitat: Areas with patches of trees for cover near open grassland. During the day, they generally rest in the shade and sheltered areas. In the evenings and early morning, they move out into open areas to graze.
              Did you know? Kangaroos can cover up to 6 m in one bound.

              Wombat

              Wombats grow up to 40 kg in weight and can reach about 115 cm head to tail. The Wombat's fur is coarse, thick and bristlelike with little or no underfur. Their fur colour varies from sandy to brown or black, to grey.

              Scientific Name: Vombatus ursinus
                Diet: Native grasses, sedges, rushes, succulent plant roots and tubers, and pasture grasses.
                  Habitat: Inhabit sub-alpine heath at altitudes of up to 1800 m, sub-alpine woodland, wet forest, dry forest, woodland, coast scrub and wet heath.
                    Did you know? Wombats often sleep with their four feet sticking up in the air!

                    Koala

                    They are covered in fine woolly fur which varies from grey to brown. Koalas have a short and stocky body. They can range up to 82 cm in length and weigh up to 14 kg.

                    Scientific Name: Phascolarctos cinereus
                      Diet: Koala's have one primary source of food, that being Eucalyptus Leaves.
                        Habitat: Koalas are widespread throughout eucalypt woodlands and forests.
                          Did you know? A Koala spends over 20 hours a day sleeping. They can also swim!
