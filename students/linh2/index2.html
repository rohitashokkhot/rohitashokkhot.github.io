Boulevard du Temple

The first photograph of a human

1837: A Moment Captured

Louis Daguerre captured this daguerreotype on the Boulevard du Temple in Paris, requiring an exposure time of approximately ten minutes.

Most moving traffic and pedestrians left no trace on the plate.

The Solitary Figure

In the lower left corner, a man having his boots polished stood still long enough to be immortalized.

He became the first human being ever photographed.

A Bustling Boulevard

Though the street appears empty in the image, it was actually a busy Paris thoroughfare.

The long exposure rendered the moving crowd invisible, creating an eerie stillness.

Dawn of a New Era

This photograph marked the beginning of visual documentation of human existence.

From this single moment, photography would go on to change how we remember our world.
