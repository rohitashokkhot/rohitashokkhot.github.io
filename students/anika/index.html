Interactive Experience Research

Website- Palat Tuntaopas Portfolio (palat2023.com)

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

I noticed a number of visual components that were brought to my attention as soon as I opened the website. The occurring motif of colour changes caught my attention, along with the lopsided distribution of colours, which changed dynamically as I navigated the page with my computer mouse. The website felt dynamic and alive to me because of this interactive effect, which elevated a basic browsing experience to me that was extremely captivating and immersive. I was inspired to look into it further and engage with the website more because of the colourful aspect that the colours brought.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

I started by placing the computer mouse in the middle of the page and watching how the colours changed in response to small movements. I then noted the changing colours as I moved the cursor around the page's corners in no specific order. Next, I hovered over the bold outlined words in the sentence, which drew my attention to textual changes. I also noticed that the colours changed from orange to purple, which made the experience even more exciting. Finally, I experimented with diagonal and vertical mouse movements, further seeing how the interactive parts reacted to different computer mouse movements.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time interacting with the page’s element of the colour-changing feature. While the text colour changed from pink to green in sync with the background's changes, the background would alternate between four different colours: green, blue, pink, and purple. The fluidity of these changes was interesting to watch, as the colour distribution changed regularly. Depending on where the computer mouse was positioned, the area covered by each colour increased or decreased, producing a dynamic and captivating visual effect that promoted constant involvement for me as the user.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

Throughout my two-minute encounter, the most popular activity was moving the mouse diagonally and continually moving it over every corner of the page to see the distinct and erratic changes in colour distribution. The distribution of hues changed with each cursor movement, creating a unique visual effect that seemed responsive and natural. This inspired me to try more because I was fascinated by how the small changes in mouse placement had a significant impact on the page's overall design. My attention was maintained throughout the entire thing by the constant interaction between colour and movement.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

By defying common expectations that people may have, the interactive experience appears to have the main impression of illustrating the boundless creative potential of web design. The website designer Palat Tuntaopas's goal is to produce a dynamic, captivating, and immersive experience that draws in people to truly explore the website. The idea that websites can be more than just informational platforms—they can also be artistic and experiential—is reinforced by the design, which encourages viewers to actively interact with the page rather than just watching and scrolling aimlessly through a page.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The dynamic and responsive design aspects of the interactive experience effectively convey this primary goal. Users are encouraged to explore various parts of the website by the continuously changing colours, which change regularly in response to the computer’s cursor movement, creating a sense of engagement for the user. The interactive aspect of the experience is further enhanced by the way the text changes in sync with the background, giving it a fluid and dynamic sense. The website effectively blurs the distinction between digital art and user interaction by letting users directly change the images, supporting Tuntaopas's idea of web design as an innovative and experimental medium.Because the website isn't designed to scroll, the user feels obligated to browse only on one page and keep moving their mouse pointer over it.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

In my opinion, people should spend two to seven minutes interacting with the interactive experience in order to properly enjoy its dynamic features. To see how the text changes, they should ideally move their mouse pointer around the sentence at least five times, paying attention to the bolded words. Additionally, they could explore the entire range of colour shifts and interactive effects by dragging the cursor around the website's background and corners at least 15–30 times. Their experience may be further improved by repeated interactions across several visits, since they might pick up on new details or changes in the way the design reacts to movement.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

In order to thoroughly observe and appreciate the shifting visual changes in the website, the interactive experience allows viewers to engage with it several times at a slow and relaxed pace. Users can explore a variety of colour distributions and transitions by slowly dragging their mouse across various areas of the page. Because the design is responsive, even small motions result in distinctive changes to the text and background, which allows the user to conduct lots of experimentation. This implies that the experience should be carefully examined rather than hurried through, rewarding people who take the time to engage with different components and fully appreciate its creative design.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

Similar to interactive installations or motion-responsive graphics, the experience makes references to various digital media forms including digital art and design, where images are dynamically altered in response to user interaction. Additionally, the changing colours and flowing interactions are similar to immersive media experiences, which are frequently observed in light installations and digital art shows. Furthermore, the typography in the website is connected to graphic design, in the way that the interaction highlights and changes the text through mouse movement.

10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

I should approach the research experience with curiosity. Instead of just looking at the visuals on the website, I am urged to actively participate by experimenting with various interactions, evaluating how small adjustments made through computer mouse movement impact the visual components of the webpage. The typography and graphic design components within the website also emphasise the value of paying close attention to details, which motivates me to concentrate on the small interactions that add to the overall user experience.

11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

According to these references, my research experience should create feelings of interest, curiosity, and engagement. As fresh colours and are revealed with each mouse movement, the dynamic, constantly shifting nature of the website graphics creates an emotional response of delight and intrigue. This ongoing change keeps the website from being monotonous or repetitive, which helped me stay focused and avoid boredom. Furthermore, the interactive components foster a sense of exploration, elevating the interaction from a passive observation to an artistic investigation.

12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

Regardless matter how the user moves their computer mouse, the webpage's uneven colour dispersion is the most frustrating aspect of the interaction. Because of the way the interactive features reacted to cursor movements, some parts of the page consistently stayed uneven, regardless of how hard I tried to achieve a balanced or symmetrical colour spread. Because the bold outlined words also have an uneven colour distribution, this problem is very apparent even when the mouse is positioned in the centre of the screen.

13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The ability to move the mouse pointer anywhere on the screen and continuously observe a dynamic change in colour distribution is the most fulfilling aspect of the interaction. Since each movement provides a different visual effect, this ongoing transition creates curiosity and engagement. Users get a sense of control over the page's changing appearance because to the responsive design, which makes the interaction feel immersive. Furthermore, each interaction feels new because of the element of surprise added by the unpredictable colour shifts. This constantly changing aspect of the experience keeps the website from being monotonous.
